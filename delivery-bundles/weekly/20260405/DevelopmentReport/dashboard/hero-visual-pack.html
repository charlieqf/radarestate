Key Reads

Leaders are selective, not noisy

Penrith currently leads because opportunity is high while friction remains low. This is very different from simply ranking by application volume.

Risk is rewriting the shortlist

Bankstown shows how a busy precinct can still become cautionary once flood, bushfire or process friction start stacking together.

Sydney and Hunter are different markets

Sydney currently has 0 A-rated precincts, while Hunter has 0. Hunter is viable, but it behaves more like a watchlist market than a clean conviction market.

Flood and bushfire dominate current risk

The current stack is being shaped much more by flood metadata and bushfire spatial hits than by biodiversity. That is where ranking changes are happening most often.
